About Me

There's nothing I love more than coming back from classes to a steaming hot homemade dinner, but it's difficult to get that especially if you're not living with your family (or you don't have a live-in chef).

Luckily, there are a few recipes I've learned that could help somewhat replicate that experience! I'd love to share them with all of you because my ultimate goal is for you to become a better cook than me, so I can start crashing your meals :D

My Life

I know you're all asking this question, how did I learn these recipes?

Growing Up

My parents had long commutes and would usually not come home from work before us, so my sister and I were typically responsible for dinner.

While my sister went on to become a great cook (+baker) and eventually opened her own bakery, I became an expert in boiling water and stirring the pot.

High School

When I was 16, I decided to move to the U.S. to finish high school. My parents sent me to this semi-boarding high school, and I lived in a dorm with 15 other girls. While we did have a small dining hall situation, the food was low-quality, to put it lightly. To defend ourselves from bone-dry meatballs and stale pasta (seriously, how do you mess up pasta?!), we all learned to cook a few simple recipes.

This is when I gained most of my cooking skills. Although my roommate started taking quite an interest in meal-prepping and I slowly got demoted to being her assistant. I'm not complaining tho, her food was bomb!
